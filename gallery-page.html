The Gorey Gallery

Welcome to the Gallery: a visual exploration of horror’s haunting presence across New York and New Jersey. This page is designed to showcase the eerie intersections between cinematic terror and regional geography, divided into four curated categories that reflect the diverse landscapes and cultural textures of these states.

“Bloodbath in the Big Apple” brings the urban dread of New York City into focus, highlighting how its density, anonymity, and towering architecture amplify horror’s psychological effects. “Scary New Jersey Suburbs” shifts attention to seemingly safe, residential neighborhoods, where horror thrives on familiarity turned sinister. In “Iconically Isolated NY and NJ,” we venture into rural woods, desolate highways, and forgotten towns. Finally, “Anomalies & Apocalypses Around Local Spaces” examines how monstrous or apocalyptic disruptions reframe the ordinary landscapes we think we know.

These categories invite you to see horror not just as entertainment, but as a mirror reflecting social anxieties embedded in place. As you explore, consider how regional identity shapes fear—how a subway tunnel, a suburban cul-de-sac, or a wooded trail can all become sites of terror. Through this gallery, you’ll gain a deeper appreciation for how New York and New Jersey have inspired horror stories that are as unsettling as they are rooted in the world around us.
